About Bill

Biographical Sketch

Bill joined the University of California, Santa Barbara in September 2010 as an incoming graduate student. He previously received his undergraduate degrees in Physics and Mathematics from his home state of Illinois at Eastern Illinois University where he was a presidential scholar and graduated summa cum laude in 2010. He also spent time at the University of Rochester in the summer of 2009 on an NSF sponsored research experience for undergraduates under the direction of Professor Eric Blackman.

Research

My primary interests currently lie in the physics of accreting white dwarf stars. White dwarfs are the compact, degenerate remains of a star that wasn't massive enough to die in a core-collapse supernovae. When in a binary system with another star, white dwarfs may accrete material, which can be burnt episodically in novae or continuously to form a very bright and hot object. I'm interested in explaining the phenomena associated with these systems, like novae, supersoft sources, and radio novae.

I am more generally interested in stellar astrophysics and supernovae, as well as development of the open source stellar evolution code, MESA. I also continue to do work in the area of superluminous supernovae, and in the past, I have studied jet formation in accretion disks, conjugacy in hyperbolic groups (my undergraduate mathematics thesis), and comptonization in neutron star atmospheres.
